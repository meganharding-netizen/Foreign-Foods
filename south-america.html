South America

About

South American food is a diverse fusion of indigenous ingredients like potatoes, corn, and beans with influences from Spanish, African, and Asian cultures, resulting in a cuisine rich in flavor and color. It features a variety of staple dishes, from grilled meats and seafood to stews and corn-based preparations, with regional variations like Argentine barbecue, Peruvian ceviche, and Brazilian stews. Meals often center around a substantial afternoon lunch, and snacks like empanadas are common.
