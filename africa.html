Africa

About

African food is diverse and regional, characterized by a wide use of locally grown vegetables, grains, and fruits, along with meat, fish, and dairy products. Common ingredients include tubers like yam and cassava, legumes, and grains such as maize, millet, and sorghum, which are often prepared in stews, soups, and porridges. Bold flavors are achieved through rich spices, herbs, and a variety of cooking techniques, with many traditional diets being plant-based.
